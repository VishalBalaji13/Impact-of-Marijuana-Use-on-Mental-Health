# Impact-of-Marijuana-Use-on-Mental-Health

This project investigates the relationship between marijuana use and mental health outcomes using data from the National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES). As legalization expands, understanding this link is essential to inform public health actions and policies. By analyzing demographic variables, substance use frequency, and mental health indicators, the study aims to provide data-driven insights into how marijuana consumption correlates with conditions like anxiety and depression.

## Data Source and Parameters

The analysis utilizes the NHANES dataset, which gathers detailed health and nutrition data from a wide range of Americans. 

The study focuses on adult populations and analyzes the following key parameters:
* **Demographics:** Age, gender, and ethnicity.
* **Substance Use:** Marijuana use (frequency and quantity).
* **Mental Health Indicators:** Anxiety, depression, and overall mental well-being scores.

## Methodology and Statistical Analysis

To evaluate the association between marijuana usage and mental health indicators, the project employs several statistical methods while adjusting for potential confounders:

* **Descriptive Statistics:** Summarizes the demographic and health characteristics of the study population by calculating means, medians, standard deviations, and interquartile ranges for continuous variables, alongside proportions for categorical variables.
* **Correlation Analysis:** Assesses the direct relationship between marijuana use and mental health indicators using Pearson or Spearman correlation coefficients.
* **Multiple Regression Analysis:** Employs linear regression models to explore the independent impact of marijuana usage on mental health outcomes after controlling for confounding variables such as age, gender, and ethnicity.
* **Subgroup Analysis:** Conducts stratified analyses to examine how different age groups, genders, and ethnicities have varied associations with marijuana use and mental health.

## Hypothesis and Findings

* **Hypothesis:** It is hypothesized that higher marijuana usage is linked to worse mental health outcomes, including increased anxiety and depressive symptoms.
* **Findings:** The analysis found that high marijuana usage has a statistically significant negative link with mental health markers, suggesting that frequent users exhibit higher levels of anxiety and depressive symptoms.
